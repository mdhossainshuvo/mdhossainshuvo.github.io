I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at Virginia Tech. I am conducting my research under the supervision of my advisor, Dr. Debswapna Bhattacharya. My research interests are,

>Computational Biology
>Applied Machine Learning
>Data Science in Bioinformatics

I am interested in developing computational methods for macromolecules to understand their structures and functions. I am particularly interested in applying data-driven machine-learning approaches, specifically those based on deep learning, for estimating the accuracy of computational models of protein molecules to understand their structural reliability (also known as: protein model quality estimation). I am also interested in harnessing the power of deep learning and optimization algorithms to improve the structural accuracy of computationally generated protein models for optimizing their reliability (also known as: protein model refinement). Therefore, the objectives of my current research are twofold: to gain insight into protein molecules in the absence of experimental data by analyzing the structural properties of their computational models and to improve their structural accuracy to align more closely with the experimental structures.

I am a recepient of the Pratt Fellowship Award, 2023 at Virginia Tech. I am also the recipient of the 1st place prize in the Young Scientist Excellence Award competition (MCBIOS, 2022) as well as multiple conference and travel fellowships.

Prior to starting my Ph.D. at Virginia Tech, I was actively conducting my research as a Ph.D. student at Auburn University (2018 - 2021). I received my Master's degree from Alabama A&M University (2017) and Bachelor's degree from Bangladesh University of Business and Technology (2014).
